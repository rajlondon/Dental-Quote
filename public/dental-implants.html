The Complete Guide to Dental Implants

Everything you need to know about dental implants, procedures, costs, and what to expect for treatment in Istanbul.

Dr. Sarah Johnson
• April 20, 2025 • 15 min read

What Are Dental Implants?

Dental implants are titanium posts that are surgically placed into the jawbone beneath your gums. Once in place, they allow your dentist to mount replacement teeth onto them. Unlike dentures or bridges, implants become a permanent part of your jaw, providing a stable foundation for artificial teeth that look, feel, and function like natural teeth.

The Benefits of Dental Implants

  • Natural look and feel: Dental implants are designed to look, feel, and function like your natural teeth.
  • Improved speech: Unlike poorly fitted dentures that can slip and cause you to mumble or slur words, implants allow you to speak naturally.
  • Comfortable fit: Because they become part of you, implants eliminate the discomfort of removable dentures.
  • Easier eating: Sliding dentures can make chewing difficult. Dental implants function like your own teeth, allowing you to eat your favorite foods with confidence.
  • Durability: With proper care, many implants last a lifetime.
  • Improved oral health: Unlike tooth-supported bridges, dental implants don't require reducing other teeth. This leaves more of your own teeth intact, improving long-term oral health.
  • Convenience: Removable dentures are just that — removable. Dental implants eliminate the embarrassment of removing dentures and the need for messy adhesives.

Types of Dental Implants

There are two main types of dental implants:

  1. Endosteal implants: These are surgically implanted directly into the jawbone. Once the surrounding gum tissue has healed, a second surgery is needed to connect a post to the original implant. Finally, an artificial tooth (or teeth) is attached to the post individually, or grouped on a bridge or denture.
  2. Subperiosteal implants: These consist of a metal frame that is fitted onto the jawbone just below the gum tissue. As the gums heal, the frame becomes fixed to the jawbone. Posts, which are attached to the frame, protrude through the gums. As with endosteal implants, artificial teeth are then mounted to the posts.

The Dental Implant Procedure

The dental implant process typically involves several stages spread over 3-9 months:

  1. Initial consultation and planning: Your dentist will conduct a thorough examination, take X-rays, and create a treatment plan tailored to your specific needs.
  2. Implant placement surgery: The dental implant (titanium post) is placed into the jawbone where the tooth is missing. This surgery usually takes 1-2 hours per implant.
  3. Healing and osseointegration: The jawbone grows around the implanted metal post, anchoring it securely in the jaw. This process, called osseointegration, takes 3-6 months.
  4. Abutment placement: Once osseointegration is complete, an abutment (extension) is attached to the implant. In some cases, this is done during the initial implant placement.
  5. Crown placement: After your gums heal, impressions of your mouth are made and used to create a custom crown (the visible part of the tooth). The crown is then attached to the abutment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care and maintenance, dental implants can last a lifetime. The crown attached to the implant typically lasts 10-15 years before it might need replacement due to wear and tear.

Is the dental implant procedure painful?

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you shouldn't feel pain during the surgery. Some discomfort, swelling, and bruising might be experienced during the healing process, but this can be managed with prescription or over-the-counter pain medication.

How many trips to Turkey will I need for dental implants?

Typically, you'll need 2-3 trips depending on the complexity of your case. The first trip (5-7 days) is for the implant placement, and the second trip (3-5 days) is for placing the final crown, usually 3-6 months later. Some clinics offer same-day or immediate-loading implants that might reduce the number of trips needed.

Are dental implants in Turkey safe?

Yes, when performed by qualified professionals in accredited clinics. The best Turkish dental clinics use the same high-quality implant brands (like Straumann, Nobel Biocare) as clinics in Western Europe and the US, and follow strict sterilization protocols. Through MyDentalFly, you'll be connected only with verified clinics that meet international standards.

What is the success rate of dental implants?

Dental implants have a success rate of about 95-98%. Factors that can affect this include the patient's overall health, whether they smoke, and their commitment to good oral hygiene and regular dental check-ups.
